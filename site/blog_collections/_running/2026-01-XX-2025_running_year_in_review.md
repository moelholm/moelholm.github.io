My second 100-miler! Unlike Kullamannen 2024 which was all about trails and mountains, Mors was a flat, super runnable road race around the beautiful island of Mors. ### Kullamannen by UTMB (November, Sweden ğŸ‡¸ğŸ‡ª)
Back to Kullamannen for the second year in a row! This time I ran the full 100 miles (172.7K) and faced one of my toughest challenges yet. Starting in darkness at 18:00, I ran through 13 hours of night before seeing daylight. The Swedish autumn weather, technical trails, and strategic dropbag placement (only one, at 120K!) made this race exceptionally demanding. After 26h41m, I earned my second Kullamannen ring and 4 more UTMB running stones. My son Casper running the last meters with me to the finish line made it all worth it.

# Reflections on 2025

2025 was a year of consolidation and ultra-distance mastery. Rather than chasing speed PBs, I focused on building endurance and tackling longer, more technical races. The three 100-mile races taught me so much about:

- **Nutrition management** over extreme distances (especially after my stomach issues at Mors)
- **Mental resilience** through 26+ hours of running
- **Night running** and managing energy through darkness
- **Technical trail skills** on challenging mountain terrain
- **Self-sufficiency** with limited aid stations (Kullamannen's single dropbag at 120K!)

The consistent high training volume (averaging 93K per week) gave me the base to tackle these challenges. And completing all 14 planned races without a DNF was a huge achievement that I'm incredibly proud of.
