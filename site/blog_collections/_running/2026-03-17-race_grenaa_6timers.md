The alarm went off at 04:40 🌅😅. Yes, you read that right - 04:40am! Parking in Grenaa is limited and I wanted to secure a good spot before the race start at 09:00, so I had to be there before 08:00. Nothing like an early morning drive to get the legs going before they actually have to go for 6 hours! 😬

The race started in race director Kenneth Pedersen's garden, which set the tone perfectly for the whole event - super relaxed, super cosy, and just a very welcoming atmosphere 🤗. Not what you typically expect when you're about to torture yourself for 6 hours straight! Around **60 runners** had gathered to take on the challenge - a great turnout!

The course itself was a blue forest route of 5.5 kilometers per loop. We were very lucky with the weather: mild temperatures, slightly cloudy at the start but gradually becoming more and more sunny throughout the day ☀️. Really ideal conditions for running long - no wind, no rain, no frozen anything. Just beautiful Danish forest vibes 🌲.

For the first time ever, I managed to successfully execute an **almost negative split** pace strategy - and I'm absolutely thrilled about it 🤩! The plan was to start at a comfortable controlled pace and then progressively push harder as the race went on. After the first 2 hours I cranked it up a notch, and then after 4 hours I pushed it just a little more again. Looking back, those last two 2-hour blocks probably converged on just below 5:00/km pace in reality.

I honestly would not have thought I'd be able to hold a sub-5:00/km pace in the 50-60 kilometer range - that's just not territory I've been in before! 😲 I think my recent marathon training combined with building more running volume overall has had a genuinely positive effect on my ability to recruit and maintain power deep into a long effort. It really showed today.

Along the way I also set a new **50K PB of 4:06:56** 🎉🥳 - which was a very nice bonus! When you're running a timed race and you realize mid-race that you're on course for a personal best at an arbitrary distance, it just adds a little extra spring to your step.

After 6 hours on the clock, I crossed the line with **{{ page.distance_km }} kilometers** covered - finishing **2nd out of 60 finishers** 🏅! To say I'm happy with that result would be a massive understatement! The volunteers and race organisation were fantastic throughout - always a helping hand at the aid station, always a smile, always enthusiastic encouragement every single lap. A huge thank you to Kenneth and the whole team 👏🏻🙌.

### Recommended?

Absolutely, without hesitation! ✅ The race has a wonderful, friendly atmosphere, a beautiful forest loop, a great race director in Kenneth Pedersen, and it's really well organised. Whether you're gunning for a podium finish or just want to see how far your legs can carry you in 6 hours, this race is a gem 💎.

I'll definitely be back 🤠👍🏻.
